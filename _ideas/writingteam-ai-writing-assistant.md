# Why AI Writing Assistants Sound Generic (and What to Do About It)

## The Idea
AI writing tools are trained on millions of samples of average writing. The result is output that sounds competent but generic — it reads like something anyone could have written. The solution isn't a better AI model; it's better instructions. Specifically: a detailed description of your own voice, your patterns, your rules, and your examples, given to the AI before it writes anything.

## Why It's Interesting
It reframes the AI writing problem. Most people approach it as "the AI doesn't write well enough yet." But the better diagnosis is "the AI doesn't know enough about me yet." The fix is documentation, not waiting for models to improve. That insight is practical, actionable, and somewhat counterintuitive — we're used to thinking of AI capability as a function of the model, not the instructions.

## Key Points to Cover
- Why generic AI output happens: trained on diverse samples, produces average output
- The common response: try different models, use more prompts, hope for better results
- The better response: document your voice thoroughly and give that documentation to the AI
- What a voice profile contains: tone, sentence habits, vocabulary preferences, what to avoid, examples
- The result: AI output that sounds like you, not like "competent AI"
