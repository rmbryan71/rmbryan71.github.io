# The Recovery Code Problem Nobody Talks About

## The Idea
When you turn on two-factor authentication (the extra security step that sends a code to your phone), most services give you a set of "recovery codes" to save in case you lose access to your phone. Almost nobody saves them properly. This is a quiet, widespread crisis waiting to happen.

## Why It's Interesting
Two-factor authentication is widely recommended and widely misunderstood. People turn it on thinking they're done. The recovery codes — the backup for when the backup fails — get screenshotted, emailed to yourself, or stuffed into a notes app. Or lost entirely. When your phone is stolen or breaks, you find out you're actually locked out of your own accounts. This is a human problem that technology created and mostly hasn't solved.

## Key Points to Cover
- What two-factor authentication is and why it matters (quick, plain-language explainer)
- What recovery codes are: one-time backup passwords if you lose your second factor
- Where people actually store them: screenshot in camera roll, note in their phone, nowhere
- What happens when you lose them and your phone at the same time
- Better options: password manager notes, printed and stored physically, dedicated authenticator app with backup
