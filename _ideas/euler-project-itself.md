# The Website That Makes Math Feel Like a Game

## The Idea
Project Euler is a website with over 800 math problems. Solving each one correctly gives you access to a forum where others share their approaches. There are no hints, no partial credit, and no time limit — just a number input box and a submit button. And yet tens of thousands of people around the world work on these problems, sometimes spending weeks on a single one.

## Why It's Interesting
It's a case study in intrinsic motivation: people do this for no external reward. No grade, no certificate worth anything, no salary attached. The appeal is the problems themselves — and the community that forms around solving them. Understanding what makes Project Euler compelling sheds light on why some challenges attract devoted effort while others with clear incentives don't.

## Key Points to Cover
- What Project Euler is and how it works (brief)
- The reward structure: just the satisfaction of solving it and access to others' solutions
- The global leaderboard and community: people from all backgrounds, all countries
- What the problems have in common: they reward insight over raw computation
- The experience of spending weeks on one problem — and what that feels like when it breaks open
