# What Happens When Stories Can Watch You Read Them

## The Idea
A branching story platform that tracks reader choices — which path they took, where they stopped, which decisions 80% of readers made — transforms storytelling from a monologue into a feedback loop. Authors can see not just that people read their story, but how, and where the story lost them. That's a fundamentally new relationship between writer and reader.

## Why It's Interesting
It applies data thinking to one of the oldest human activities. Authors have always wondered what readers experienced — now, in interactive fiction, you can actually know. This raises interesting questions about what authors should do with that data, and whether knowing that 80% of readers chose the same path means the other paths were wasted effort or valuable texture.

## Key Points to Cover
- What analytics look like in a branching story: choice counts per decision, drop-off rates per scene, completed paths
- What you can learn: where readers lose interest, which choices feel easy vs. agonizing, which paths are underused
- What you can't learn from the data: whether readers enjoyed what they read, whether the "wrong" paths felt meaningful
- The design implication: knowing that 80% chose left doesn't mean right was a mistake
- The privacy consideration: tracking reader behavior, even anonymously, is a choice with implications
