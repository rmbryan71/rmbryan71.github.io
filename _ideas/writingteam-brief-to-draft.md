# Why "Make It Punchier" Is Not Useful Feedback

## The Idea
Most writing feedback is vague: "make it flow better," "tighten it up," "it's too long." These instructions are well-intentioned but nearly impossible to act on without interpretation. Converting vague feedback into specific, actionable writing instructions is a skill in its own right — and one that matters whether you're working with a human editor, a collaborator, or an AI writing tool.

## Why It's Interesting
Vague feedback is endemic in creative work and professional writing. Understanding why it's vague (the giver often knows something is wrong without knowing what) and how to convert it into something actionable (ask better questions, operationalize the intuition) is a practical skill that improves writing outcomes regardless of who's doing the writing. It also explains why AI writing assistants produce disappointing results when given vague prompts.

## Key Points to Cover
- Common vague feedback phrases and what they usually actually mean
- Why people give vague feedback: they know something is off but can't identify what
- How to interrogate vague feedback into specific direction
- The brief: what a useful writing assignment actually contains (audience, goal, constraints, examples)
- Why specificity produces better output from any writer or tool
