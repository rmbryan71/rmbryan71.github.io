# The Strange Stats That Actually Win EBL

## The Idea
EBL doesn't score home runs directly. Instead it uses a formula that rewards "total bases plus walks plus hit-by-pitches plus stolen bases" for hitters, and counts outs recorded (not strikeouts or ERA) for pitchers. Understanding why these stats were chosen reveals a smarter way of thinking about what baseball players actually contribute.

## Why It's Interesting
Most casual fans think home runs and batting average are the whole story. But baseball analysts have spent decades figuring out which stats actually predict winning. EBL's scoring formula is based on that research. A batter who draws a walk looks boring on TV but scores exactly as many EBL points as a batter who gets hit by a pitch — because both got on base. This makes for surprising weekly leaders.

## Key Points to Cover
- What the offensive formula measures: total bases (1 for single, 2 for double, etc.) + walks + hit by pitch + stolen bases
- Why walks count: getting on base without swinging is valuable
- What the pitching formula measures: outs recorded, not strikeouts or wins
- Why outs matter: every out a pitcher records is one fewer out a reliever has to get
- Surprising outcomes: a pitcher who throws 7 innings of average baseball may outscore a dominant 3-inning closer
