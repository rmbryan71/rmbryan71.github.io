# When One Breach Becomes Ten

## The Idea
When a company you use gets hacked and your password is stolen, the real danger isn't that company — it's every other site where you used the same password. A single breach at a minor website can cascade into lost access to your email, your bank, and your social accounts. This is the actual mechanics of how most people get "hacked."

## Why It's Interesting
Most people think of getting hacked as something that happens to them directly — someone targets them specifically. In reality, most account takeovers are collateral damage from breaches at companies the victim barely remembers using. The attack is automated and impersonal. Understanding this changes the conversation from "why would anyone want to hack me?" to "I'm on a list along with millions of others."

## Key Points to Cover
- How data breaches work: a company's database is stolen and sold
- What attackers do with it: automated tools try those credentials everywhere
- Why password reuse turns one breach into many

- The specific cascade: email → password reset → everything else
