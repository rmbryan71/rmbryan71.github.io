# The Statistic That Rewards Showing Up

## The Idea
Offensive linemen don't throw touchdowns or run for yards. They block — and if they do it well, nothing happens (which is the point). Traditional fantasy football has no way to reward them. The solution: award points per snap played. Showing up and doing your job, play after play, becomes measurable and valuable.

## Why It's Interesting
It raises a question worth asking outside of football: what do we value that we can't easily measure? And what do we measure that becomes what we value, whether or not it's the right thing? Snap counts are an imperfect proxy for contribution — a lineman could be playing through injury or getting beat repeatedly — but they're a starting point for recognizing that presence and effort exist even when they don't show up in conventional stats.

## Key Points to Cover
- What an offensive lineman does and why it's invisible in traditional stats
- What snap counts are: a record of every play a player was on the field
- Why snap counts are a reasonable proxy for contribution, even without specific stats
- The position-specific values: offensive line snaps are worth more than running back snaps, reflecting strategic importance
- The limits of snap-based scoring: it doesn't distinguish excellent from adequate performance
