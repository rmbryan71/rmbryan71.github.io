# The Rarest Play in Football Gets the Biggest Reward

## The Idea
What if a quarterback throws a touchdown pass and then makes a tackle on the defensive play? In most fantasy systems, the tackle doesn't exist. In this scoring system, both score — and the combination is worth more than either alone. The rarest, most genuinely impressive plays get disproportionate rewards. That's not a bug; it's a design philosophy.

## Why It's Interesting
Rewarding rarity is a way of recognizing genuine excellence rather than volume. A quarterback who can make a tackle is doing something almost no one at that position does. A wide receiver who throws a touchdown pass is a one-in-a-season event. When scoring systems treat these moments the same as ordinary plays, they flatten the landscape of athletic achievement. Making rarity visible and valuable changes how we see the game.

## Key Points to Cover
- What "dual scoring" means: a player who qualifies under both offensive and defensive scoring gets both applied
- Why this rarely happens: football positions are specialized; crossing over is genuinely unusual
- Examples: a QB making a tackle, a wide receiver throwing a pass, a defensive player scoring on offense
- Why disproportionate rewards make strategic sense: you're recognizing genuine flexibility and adaptability
- The connection to real NFL value: "two-way" players are coveted for exactly this reason
