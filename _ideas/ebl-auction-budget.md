# The $100 Problem: How an Auction Draft Forces Hard Choices

## The Idea
Every team in EBL starts with exactly $100 and has to bid on 4 players from the Phillies roster in a live auction. How you spend that money determines your entire season — and you can never get it back.

## Why It's Interesting
It's a real-time economic puzzle with imperfect information. Do you spend $40 on the best player and scramble for the rest? Or spread it evenly and hope for consistency? Other teams' bids change the market in real time. There's no right answer — only tradeoffs. This is a natural entry point into concepts like budget constraints, game theory, and decision-making under pressure.

## Key Points to Cover
- The $100 budget: how much you spend on one player affects all others
- The reservation rule: you must keep $1 per remaining roster spot, so you can't go all-in early
- Bid increments ($1, $3, $5, $8) and how they shape the pace
- What happens when two teams really want the same player — a bidding war
- Real example: spending $40 on a star leaves only $20 each for three more players
