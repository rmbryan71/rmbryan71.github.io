# When a Tie Is Worth More Than a Win

## The Idea
EBL's weekly scoring system hands out points to the top three teams each week. But when teams tie, the math gets weird in ways that can flip a leaderboard overnight — and it's completely by design.

## Why It's Interesting
The tie rules are genuinely surprising. If two teams tie for first, they both get first-place points — AND no one gets second-place points. If six teams all score zero, the one team that managed a single walk wins the week outright. These edge cases reveal how scoring systems create incentives, drama, and comeback potential that wouldn't exist with simpler rules.

## Key Points to Cover
- How weekly scoring works: top 3 teams get 10, 8, 4 points; everyone else gets 0
- What happens when two teams tie for first (both get 10, no one gets 8)
- What happens when most teams score zero — a single stat wins the week
- The all-star break edge case: one player getting a single at-bat could win a week
- Why this creates volatility and makes last place less hopeless
