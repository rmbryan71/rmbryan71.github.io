# You Have More Accounts Than You Think

## The Idea
The average person has over 80 online accounts. Most people would guess 20. When you export your password manager data and actually count, the number is almost always shocking. This article explores where all those accounts came from, why so few get deleted, and what that means for your digital security.

## Why It's Interesting
Account proliferation is invisible until you count. Every time you signed up for a free trial, checked out as a guest (but created an account anyway), or registered for something you used once — that account still exists somewhere, probably with your email address and a reused password. Most people don't think of this as a security problem, but it is.

## Key Points to Cover
- The average: 80+ accounts per person, most people estimate far fewer
- Where they come from: free trials, single-use signups, e-commerce, work tools, apps
- Why old accounts don't get deleted: friction, forgotten passwords, "I might need it someday"
- Why dormant accounts are a security risk: breaches at old services expose credentials you still use elsewhere
- What you can actually do about it: periodic audits, using "Sign in with Google" to reduce proliferation
