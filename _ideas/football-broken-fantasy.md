# What's Wrong with Fantasy Football (and How to Fix It)

## The Idea
Traditional fantasy football rewards quarterbacks, wide receivers, and running backs — and largely ignores offensive linemen, linebackers, safeties, and most defensive players. That means the "fantasy" version of the game has almost nothing to do with how the real game is won. A new scoring system built around the Eagles asks: what if every player on the field actually mattered?

## Why It's Interesting
It's a design problem hiding in a familiar package. Most people accept fantasy football's scoring rules without questioning them. But those rules create a game that rewards picking elite skill positions and mostly ignores the rest of the roster. A from-scratch rethinking of those rules produces a completely different game — one that's more faithful to real football and more strategically interesting.

## Key Points to Cover
- What traditional fantasy football rewards (and ignores)
- The consequence: most roster spots are effectively decorative
- The alternative: a system where offensive linemen score for snaps, linebackers score for tackles, and every position has meaningful value
- What changes in strategy: you can't win by just hoarding quarterbacks
- The Eagles-only constraint: focusing on one team's actual roster makes the depth matter even more
