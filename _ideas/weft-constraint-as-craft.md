# Why the Best Authoring Tool Might Be One That Says No

## The Idea
Weft is a branching story tool that deliberately prevents certain structures: loops (stories that cycle back infinitely), dead ends (scenes with no exit), disconnected scenes (scenes no choice ever reaches). These aren't limitations — they're design guarantees. The tool makes a category of mistakes structurally impossible, which paradoxically makes authors more free.

## Why It's Interesting
Constraints in creative tools are usually understood as limitations. But some constraints make the work better by eliminating an entire class of error. A spell checker doesn't limit your vocabulary — it lets you write without thinking about spelling. A tool that prevents dead ends doesn't limit your story structure — it lets you write without worrying about structural errors. Understanding this distinction changes how you evaluate creative tools.

## Key Points to Cover
- What Weft prevents: loops, dead ends, disconnected scenes
- Why these feel like limitations at first: authors might want loops or deliberate dead ends
- Why they're actually guarantees: you can write knowing the structure will be coherent
- The analogy: spell check, grammar check, type systems in programming — all constraints that liberate
- Where Weft draws the line: it prevents structural errors but makes no decisions about content
