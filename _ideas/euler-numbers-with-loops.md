# The Numbers That Chase Their Own Tails

## The Idea
Take the number 145. Add up the factorials of its digits: 1! + 4! + 5! = 1 + 24 + 120 = 145. You get back where you started. Some numbers create loops — chains where each step brings you back to an earlier number. Every positive integer eventually gets trapped in one of these loops. No exceptions. That's a strange and beautiful mathematical fact.

## Why It's Interesting
It's surprising. Most people have no intuition that arbitrary arithmetic operations on numbers would produce cycles — and that every number, no matter how large, eventually falls into one of just a handful of fixed loops. It's an accessible introduction to the idea that number systems have hidden structure, and that exploring that structure can reveal astonishing patterns.

## Key Points to Cover
- What a factorial is (plain language: 5! = 5 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 = 120)
- The specific loop: 145 → 145 (it maps to itself — a loop of length 1)
- A longer loop: 169 → 363601 → 1454 → 169 (a cycle of length 3)
- The remarkable fact: every number eventually hits a cycle — none escape
- Why this matters: it's a window into the hidden structure of numbers
