# When What You Buy Determines What You Can Do

## The Idea
Most games separate "getting your stuff" from "using your stuff." In this card game, the auction phase — where you bid on equipment — directly determines your options in the battle phase. Spend too much on one item and you can't afford what you need later. It creates a game where financial decisions and tactical decisions are the same decision.

## Why It's Interesting
This design pattern — where resource allocation determines capability — mirrors real-world situations constantly. A startup that spends all its funding on one product can't pivot. A football team that blows its salary cap on one player has to field cheaper talent everywhere else. Understanding how the game connects spending to capability makes those real-world dynamics easier to see and reason about.

## Key Points to Cover
- How the auction works: limited budget, bid on equipment in real time
- How equipment determines battle options: what you own determines what you can play
- The coupling: an early expensive choice forecloses later options
- What good auction strategy looks like: reserving options vs. committing early
- Why this feels different from games where spending and playing are separate phases
