# Why Good Editing Is Five Different Jobs

## The Idea
Most writers think of editing as one thing: fixing what's wrong. But professional editing breaks into at least five distinct roles — developmental editing (structure), line editing (sentence quality), copy editing (grammar and consistency), proofreading (final errors), and fact-checking. Doing all five simultaneously while also drafting is why writing feels hard. Separating them makes each one easier.

## Why It's Interesting
It explains a source of writer's block that most people don't recognize. The feeling of being stuck often comes from trying to simultaneously invent content and evaluate it — a cognitive conflict between creation and criticism. Understanding editing as a series of separate passes, each with a specific job, produces better work with less friction. It's a workflow insight that applies to any creative process.
