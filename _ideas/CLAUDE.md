# Claude Guidelines — _ideas/

Ideas are raw material. The goal at this stage is to capture and evaluate, not polish.

## When Helping with an Idea File
- Help the author articulate *what* the article is about and *why* a non-technical reader would care
- Ask: what's the one thing the reader should walk away understanding?
- Identify whether the idea is too broad (needs to be split) or too narrow (needs more substance)
- Note any obvious visual opportunities early — diagrams, charts, or illustrations that could anchor the piece

## Idea File Format
Each idea file should contain:
```
# [Working Title]

## The Idea
One or two sentences: what is this about?

## Why It's Interesting
Why would a non-technical reader care?

## Key Points to Cover
- Bullet list of the main things to explain

## Potential Visuals
- Any charts, diagrams, or illustrations that might help

## Notes / Open Questions
Anything unresolved or to research later
```

## What to Flag
- Ideas that depend heavily on assumed technical knowledge — note what context will need to be built for the reader
- Ideas that are really multiple articles — suggest splitting
- Ideas that may be too thin to reach a coherent narrative even under 750 words
