# Branching Stories Aren't Just for Games Anymore

## The Idea
When most people hear "interactive fiction," they think of games. But the same branching narrative structure is used in corporate training simulations, medical decision-making tools, emergency response scenario planning, and educational content. Weft is designed for storytellers, but the structural problem it solves is the same one faced by anyone designing a decision-based experience.

## Why It's Interesting
It broadens the concept of "story" to include anywhere humans navigate choices with consequences. A patient working through a medical decision aid is following a branching path. A new employee training on how to handle a difficult conversation is following a branching path. Understanding interactive fiction as a form of decision architecture makes it relevant to far more people than the "it's for games" framing suggests.

## Key Points to Cover
- What branching narrative looks like outside of fiction: training simulations, medical aids, scenario planning
- What these use cases have in common: structured choices with meaningful consequences
- Where the authoring challenges are the same: dead ends, path explosion, consistency across branches
- Where they differ: stakes (a wrong branch in a training simulation has real-world consequences)
- The opportunity: tools designed for fiction authors that work equally well for instructional designers
