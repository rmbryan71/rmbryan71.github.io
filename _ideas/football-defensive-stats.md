# What Does a Linebacker Actually Do for Eight Points?

## The Idea
In traditional fantasy football, a linebacker who makes 8 tackles in a dominant game scores zero points. In a redesigned scoring system calibrated to real NFL data, that same performance is worth 8 points — more than a wide receiver who catches one ball. This is a story about how we measure athletic contribution, and how the numbers we choose to count shape what we value.

## Why It's Interesting
Measurement shapes behavior and perception. When we don't count something, we implicitly say it doesn't matter. Football has been quantifying offensive performance for decades while defensive statistics remained crude (tackles, sacks) or ignored entirely. A scoring system that takes defense seriously reveals how much of the game has been invisible to most fans.

## Key Points to Cover
- Why defense has historically been hard to quantify (no simple "yards gained" equivalent)
- What the new system measures: tackles, sacks weighted 3x higher than QB hurries, turnovers forced, defensive touchdowns
- Why a safety's tackle is worth more per play than a linebacker's (position scarcity)
- The "pressure score" concept: sacks + QB hits, weighted by how much each ends drives
- What changes when defense scores: teams that invest in defensive depth become competitive
