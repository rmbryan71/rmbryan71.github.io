# The Shortcut That Can Kill You

## The Idea
In most games, a shortcut to victory is pure upside — you take it every time. In this card game, the Pressure Cooker equipment heats you up twice as fast, but if you overshoot the target temperature, you explode. It's a trap disguised as an advantage. Understanding why that design works reveals something true about risk and reward everywhere.

## Why It's Interesting
It's a clean, relatable example of a decision where the upside is visible and the downside is easy to underestimate. The Pressure Cooker mirrors real decisions: taking out a loan to start a business, accepting a high-stress job for more pay, cutting corners on a deadline. The game makes the tradeoff explicit and immediate — and that's what makes it a teaching tool.

## Key Points to Cover
- How the game works: players race to reach a target temperature, equipment changes how fast you heat up
- What the Pressure Cooker does: heats faster, but overshooting means elimination
- Why people take it anyway: the upside is obvious, the risk feels manageable
- When it's actually worth it: if you're close enough to the finish line, the extra speed outweighs the risk
- The real-world analogy: high-reward options that carry hidden downside risk
