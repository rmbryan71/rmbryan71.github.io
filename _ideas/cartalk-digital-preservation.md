# Your Favorite Show Could Disappear Tomorrow

## The Idea
Car Talk ran for decades on NPR and became one of the most beloved radio programs in American history. Its episodes are still available — for now. But podcasts and digital audio can vanish when servers go down, licenses lapse, or organizations decide to stop hosting old content. The only reliable way to keep something you love is to keep a copy yourself.

## Why It's Interesting
Most people assume that if something is on the internet, it's permanent. It isn't. Streaming services lose licensing rights. Podcasts get pulled when hosts die or companies fold. Websites go dark. The internet is a terrible archive, and the things we love online are more fragile than they appear. This is a story about digital impermanence and the instinct to preserve things that matter.
