# The Problem That Ran Overnight and the Insight That Solved It in Seconds

## The Idea
A math puzzle that takes a computer all night to solve by brute force can sometimes be solved in seconds once you understand the right mathematical insight. Project Euler Problem 69 is a perfect example: an exhaustive search approach runs for hours, but applying Euler's Product Formula — a mathematical pattern — reduces it to a blink. The gap between those two approaches is the gap between computing power and mathematical understanding.

## Why It's Interesting
It captures something profound about problem-solving: the right idea beats raw effort. This plays out everywhere — in science, business, and everyday decisions. Throwing more resources at a hard problem often produces slow, expensive results. Finding the underlying structure of the problem produces elegant, fast ones. The overnight-to-seconds jump makes the abstract principle viscerally concrete.

## Key Points to Cover
- The problem: find the number under one million where the ratio of it to its "totient" is largest
- What a totient is (plain language: how many numbers below N share no common factors with N)
- The brute force approach: check every number one by one — runs all night
- The insight: numbers whose only prime factors are small primes have the largest ratios
- The result: the answer is just the product of the first several prime numbers — solved instantly
