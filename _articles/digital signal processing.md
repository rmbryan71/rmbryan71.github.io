Digital signal processing (DSP) is the study of analyzing, modifying, and synthesizing signals, where signals are _any_ observable change in a quantity over space or time.

Signals do not have to carry information, but they can, and DSP is way more fun when they do.

These are the most helpful resources I've found for studying DSP so far:
- [Wikipedia : Digital signal processing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Digital_signal_processing)
- [Wikipedia : Signal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Signal)
- [Think DSP](http://greenteapress.com/thinkdsp/thinkdsp.pdf) by Allen Downey
- [DSP First 2e](https://dspfirst.gatech.edu/) from Georgia Tech
- [https://jackschaedler.github.io/](https://jackschaedler.github.io/)
- [The Scientist and Engineer's Guide to Digital Signal Processing](https://dspguide.com/) by Steven Smith
- [Signal Processing with SciPy](https://docs.scipy.org/doc/scipy/reference/signal.html)
- [Understanding Digital Signal Processing](https://markusgritsch.github.io/toc.html) by Richard Lyons
